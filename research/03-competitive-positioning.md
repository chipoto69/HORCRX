# 03 — Competitive Positioning

HORCRX should be positioned as a **vessel primitive**, not as another service directory. That distinction matters because most adjacent surfaces in this ecosystem sell execution access, not portable agent state.

## Core distinction

- **Service-as-primitive** marketplaces sell access to a running endpoint, tool, or skill.
- **Vessel-as-primitive** marketplaces sell the portable artifact itself: soul, skills, memory canon, traces, governance rules, and lineage metadata.

HORCRX belongs in the second category.

## Why the local stack creates an opening

The local ATLAS material already treats agents as priced, discoverable market actors, but the concrete thing being sold is still closer to a service listing than to a portable self. The gap between `/Users/rudlord/.claude-worktrees/ATLAS/knowledge-horcrux/atlas/marketplace/` and `/Users/rudlord/.hermes/profiles/` is exactly the opening HORCRX occupies.

## Positioning statement

HORCRX is the protocol layer underneath service discovery:

1. a vessel can be bought once and instantiated many times,
2. a vessel can be forked with lineage intact,
3. a vessel can export from Hermes and import into another compatible runtime,
4. a service listing can point at a vessel, but a vessel is not reducible to a service listing.
