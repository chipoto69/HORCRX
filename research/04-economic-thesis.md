# 04 — Economic Thesis

HORCRX only makes sense if the vessel becomes a priced unit of value rather than a decorative wrapper. The economic case across the local research breaks into four linked claims.

## 1. Consumption pricing will replace seat pricing for agent-native software

The Aaron Levie thesis captured in the local wiki is that headless, agent-consumable software moves pricing toward consumption. HORCRX fits that frame because a vessel can monetize usage, not just ownership. A buyer might acquire the vessel once, then pay ongoing x402-metered fees as the vessel performs work or calls premium skills. ## 2. Default derivative economics should be explicit

The sorrywecan precedent surfaced in the wiki recon is a default 70/20/10 split: creator / resonators / protocol. HORCRX should treat that as a research-backed default, not as immutable canon. The important part is explicit derivative flow, because vessels are meant to be forked. Sources: `/Users/rudlord/.factory/missions/df2673b6-89f8-4626-b2b1-0857353d356c/library/W-C-wiki-deep-dive.md`, `/Users/rudlord/wiki/raw/reader/Keeping What's Yours in the Age of Autonomous Corporations (01jwfbnr0p3qzcd2xsdp8xsmxb).md`.

## 3. IP becomes a first-class operating cost in agent businesses

The Goodalexander / Autonomous Corporation thread argues that IP becomes line item number one when agents scale. HORCRX aligns with that thesis by turning soul, memory canon, and specialized skill packs into assets that can be licensed, sold, and preserved. Sources: `/Users/rudlord/wiki/raw/reader/Keeping What's Yours in the Age of Autonomous Corporations (01jwfbnr0p3qzcd2xsdp8xsmxb).md`, `/Users/rudlord/wiki/raw/downloads/2026-04-10/root/codevault-landing-copy.md`.

## 4. The protocol supports both agent vessels and human creative work

The research consistently points toward a dual market:

| Surface | What is being paid for | Why HORCRX fits |
|---|---|---|
| Agent vessel | portable operating state, skills, identity, and upgrade path | vessels can be instantiated, audited, forked, and recomposed |
| Human creative work | timestamped authorship, style packets, preserved notebooks, code or voice archives | the same signed, content-addressed packaging primitives still apply |

## Operating revenue planes

1. **Primary sale** — purchase of a vessel or preservation artifact.
2. **Usage** — x402-metered execution, premium skill calls, or hosted upgrades.
3. **Fork royalties** — downstream derivative payment when a vessel is remixed.
4. **Preservation services** — notarization, signing, storage, and retrieval for human IP.

## Why this is more than marketplace theater

The operator wiki repeatedly warns that agent-economy talk becomes empty when it stops at slides. The value in HORCRX is that every revenue claim maps to a concrete local substrate already present: x402 for usage, ATLAS marketplace for discovery patterns, Hermes for runtime instantiation, and Story-like reasoning for derivative flows.
